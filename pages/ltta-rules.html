General Info

  • LTTA operates under the oversight of the Coulee Region Tennis Association.
  • Match location: Green Island Park, 2300 7th St South, La Crosse, WI.
  • Matches are scheduled for Tuesday and Wednesday evenings from June 3 – August 14 at 5:30pm and 7:00pm.

Team Captain Responsibilities

  • Each team must have a Team Captain. A Co-Captain is strongly recommended.
  • The Captain communicates team needs to the League Coordinator.
  • Collect league fees from players and submit them by the second week of play.
  • Check and adjust line-ups and subs on score sheets before each match.
  • Review and total scores after matches. Sign or initial score sheets.
  • Represent the team at pre-season meetings or assign a team representative.
  • Help new players understand league rules and match format.
  • If a team skips a summer but wants to return the following year, the spot will be held temporarily if the Captain communicates this in advance.

Team Member Responsibilities

  • Arrive 10 minutes before your scheduled match time.
  • Find your own substitute using the sub list or a player from another night with similar skills.
  • #3 players may sub for #2; #4 and #5 are interchangeable with no penalty.
  • If you play more than one level up, your ranking may be reviewed if you win.
  • Playing down a level (outside #4 exception) results in a 10–4 point split against your team.
  • Notify the League Coordinator before a match when using a new or unsanctioned sub.
  • You may only be on one night’s team unless there's a shortage at your level. Double participation requires double fees.
  • Each player must pay the $25 league fee to the Captain by Week 2 (covers balls/courts).
  • To keep your spot during a one-year break, inform your Captain and the League Coordinator.
  • Wear appropriate attire (shirt and shoes). Behave with good tennis etiquette—be a role model for younger spectators.

Match Play Rules

  • Match times: 5:30pm and 7:00pm. Be ready for warm-up no later than 10 minutes before match time.
  • Warm-up is limited to 10 minutes total. No "First Ball In" serving.
  • Matches must end on time. 5:30pm matches should finish by 7:00pm. Incomplete matches may be finished offsite, same night only, using personal balls.
  • If a match is halted, determine winner by total games won. Winner receives 10 points, opponent 8. If tied, both get 8 points.
  • Use No-Ad scoring (15, 30, 40, deuce). On deuce, receiver picks the side, next point wins the game.
  • Tiebreakers (set or third-set): first to 7 points, win by 2. Use standard tiebreak rotation and side changes.
  • If heat index is above 95°F, matches may start at 2-2. If over 104°F, play is canceled. Coordinator will decide by 4:30pm. Players can still choose to play, but results won’t count.
  • League play continues despite poor air quality. Players can wear masks or sub out.
  • Each session (5:30 or 7:00) may be canceled independently based on weather.

Scoring System

Each match awards up to 10 points. Use the following point system:

  • 10 points – Match winner by total games (e.g., 6–4, 6–3 or 6–4, 3–4 with more total games won).
  • 8 points – Non-winner in a split set (e.g., 6–2, 3–6, 5–7 tiebreak).
  • 8 points – Both teams in a halted match with tied games (e.g., 6–4, 4–6, can't finish 3rd set).
  • 8 points – Non-winner in a halted match but with fewer games (e.g., 4–6, 4–3).
  • 6 points – Non-winner of a completed match (e.g., 2–6, 1–6).
  • 6 points – Third set retiree; opponent gets 10 points.
  • 4 points – Second set retiree; opponent gets 10 points.
  • 4 points – Team using a higher-rated sanctioned player (e.g., #2 playing a #3 match); opponent gets 10 points.
  • 2 points – First set retiree; opponent gets 10 points.
  • 1 point – Team used a non-sanctioned player; opponent gets 10 points.
  • 0 points – Forfeit or late arrival; opponent gets 10 points.

See the USTA Friend at Court for final rulings.

Team Organization

  • Each team has 8 players ranked by skill: #1, #2, #3, #3, #3, #3, #4, and #5.
  • Rankings are local and do not match USTA ratings.
  • Doubles matches are formed as: #1/#2, any #3/#3 pair, and #4/#5.
  • Players must be 18+ or graduating high school seniors (must turn 18 by September of the current year).

Skill Level Descriptions (Self-Rated)

  • #1 – Anticipates well, approaches net, executes lobs, volleys, overheads consistently.
  • #2 – Adds power and spin, solid footwork, strong first serve, needs better second serve.
  • #3 – Reliable strokes, can vary shots, uses strategy and placement.
  • #4 – Less consistency and depth, can place shots but not force errors.
  • #5 – Developing player, can rally at slow pace, struggles with serve and all strokes.
